# Welcome!

I am a Research Fellow at the University of Birmingham, currently contributing to the Hydrogen Integration for Accelerated Energy Transitions (HI-ACT) project. I also serve as an Assistant Professor at Abdullah Gül University.

My research interests lie in cyber-physical systems security, in-vehicle communication networks, embedded systems, and the Internet of Things (IoT). Recently, I’ve also been exploring precision agriculture.

In addition to research, I teach undergraduate and graduate courses, particularly in embedded systems and programming. I enjoy mentoring <em> enthusiastic </em> students and engaging in interdisciplinary collaborations that connect academic research with practical applications.

Please feel free to reach out if you're interested in my work, potential collaborations, or simply wish to connect.


## Education
I earned my Ph.D. in 2022 from Cranfield University, where my research focused on the security of in-vehicle Controller Area Network (CAN) communication. Prior to that, I completed an MSc in Embedded Systems Engineering with distinction at the University of Leeds in 2016, and a BSc in Electrical and Electronics Engineering from Çukurova University in 2013. My postgraduate education was supported by a scholarship awarded by the Turkish Ministry of National Education.

## Academic Service
I contribute to the academic community as a reviewer for international journals and conferences. I also serve on the Technical Committee of the International Conference on Smart Energy Grid Engineering (SEGE).
